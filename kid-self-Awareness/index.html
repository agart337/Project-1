Self-Awareness


As children, we are not taught to prioritize our feelings. The educational system emphasizes obedience over sensitivity, constraining free thought and independent spirit. It imposes rules that do not encourage self-exploration or personal potential.

The system exists to sustain itself, limiting individuality and suppressing independent thinking. As artists, our purpose is not to conform but to develop self-understanding and awareness of the world.

Self-awareness means tuning in to our thoughts, feelings, and perceptions without interference. It refines our ability to recognize what resonates deeply within us, guiding us toward greatness.

True self-awareness is about inner experience, not external perception. The more we identify with how others see us, the more disconnected we become. Our energy should be focused inward, expanding beyond limitations.

We seek higher consciousness by releasing attachment to our perceived self. Self-awareness is transcendence—a letting go of ego. It is both tuning in to the self and surrendering the self.

As artists, we move closer to the universe by moving closer to ourselves, blurring the line between where one ends and the other begins. It is a journey from the here to the now.
