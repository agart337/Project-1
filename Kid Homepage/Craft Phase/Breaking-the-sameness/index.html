Breaking The Sameness


There are times during the Craft phase when you hit a wall. Before stepping away, it’s worth finding ways to break the sameness and refresh your excitement.



Small Steps

A blocked musician was asked to write one line daily. The quality didn’t matter—just the commitment. This small task reopened the creative channel, leading to full compositions faster than expected.



Change the Environment

Altering an element of the setting can shift consciousness. Turning off lights, changing the time of recording, or even hanging upside down while singing have produced new variations in performance.



Change the Stakes

Modifying internal stakes can impact performance. A band imagining it’s their last time playing a song may perform it differently. Conversely, lowering stakes with a rehearsal before recording can also improve results.



Invite an Audience

Being observed changes how an artist performs. Even one outsider can sharpen focus. This applies beyond music—writing or cooking may also shift with an audience present.



Change the Context

If a singer lacks connection to a song, reinterpreting its meaning can help. A love song might take on new depth when sung as a devotion to God. Experimenting with different perspectives can enhance performance.



Alter the Perspective

Adjusting volume in headphones changes dynamics. Louder playback leads to quieter performances, while lowering vocal volume can encourage stronger delivery. In concerts, altering lighting or in-ear monitor settings influences engagement.



Write for Someone Else

Imagining writing for a favorite artist can remove self-imposed limitations. Choosing an artist with a contrasting style can prevent creative stagnation.



Add Imagery

Describing a vivid scene before performing can shape musical expression. A keyboard solo improved when framed as the aftermath of a battle. Even without clear connections, imagery can strengthen direction.



Limit the Information

To avoid musicians being influenced by a demo, only basic elements like chords are shared. This allows them to interpret freely, leading to more original results.



These exercises are flexible. The goal is to shift perspectives, explore new approaches, and push beyond familiar creative methods.
