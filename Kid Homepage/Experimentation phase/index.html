The Experimentation Phase


In this phase, though you are now focused on a single seed, you are still exploring different ways of developing it. Use generative techniques like asking “what if” questions to try out various possibilities. Do the opposite of what comes naturally. Be open to surprise.

Here again, Rubin argues against deciding on a specific direction too quickly: “In this phase, we are not looking at which iteration progresses the quickest or furthest, but which holds the most promise. We focus on the flourishing and wait to prune. We generate possibilities instead of eliminating them.”

Often by trying things that might seem unconventional or even "wrong" at first glance; it's focused on exploration and openness to surprise, rather than immediate perfectionism.
