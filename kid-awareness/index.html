Awareness


In daily life, we set goals and strategies, but awareness moves differently—it unfolds around us, with the world as the doer and us as witnesses. We have little control over its content.

Awareness lets us notice the present moment without attachment or involvement, observing sensations, thoughts, feelings, and surroundings. Detached noticing allows things to reveal themselves without intervention.

Awareness isn’t forced but allowed, requiring persistence. It is presence and acceptance of the now. The moment we analyze, we shift from noticing to studying. Awareness comes first as pure connection; understanding follows.

While we can’t change what we notice, we can change how we notice. Awareness can expand or narrow, focus inward or outward, zoom in or out, altering perception.

The universe is only as vast as our awareness. Expanding awareness expands the universe, broadening both our creative potential and the life we experience.
